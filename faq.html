Frequently Asked Questions

Who writes the questions? Are they reliable?
Questions are written by medical students. We strongly encourage everyone to write questions as it is a great way to revise for exams. Occasionally questions will contain mistakes; however we have systems in place to minimise this risk:
  • Anyone can report issues with a question if they think there is a mistake. Questions with outstanding issues are clearly marked, and the person who wrote the question will be notified.
  • If someone is posting questions with mistakes, or you simply don't like their style of questions you can block that person. You will no longer be asked any questions written by them.
Why should I choose this instead of some other question bank?
We aren't trying to compete with the big question banks such as Pastest and OnExamination - we use them and think they're great. However Student Question Banks offers quite a bit that they don't:
  • Questions are written by students on your course and linked to your lectures. You can be sure that you are revising what you need to know for your exam.
  • You can write your own questions and self-test, replacing the need for flash cards (which are always getting lost and quickly become outdated).
  • It's cheap - just £10 per year, much less than most larger companies
  • You can try the site for free with no limitations before paying a penny
Are you endorsed by my university?
This site is run privately and hence is not officially endorsed by any medical school. However our King's College site has the unofficial blessing of the university's Medical Education Committee.
