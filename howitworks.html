How it works


All the mechanics of how the question banks work

We are to other question banks as Wikipedia is to the Encyclopaedia Brittanica.

  • A student from your university signs up for a free trial, using their university email to confirm that they are a genuine student.
  • They will then have full access to thousands of questions written by fellow students on their course. These will be either flash card style question/answers or MCQs. They can be browsed by year, by topic, or by individual lecture. Practice quizzes can be created based on any part of the course you choose, and you can track your progress.
  • Students can post their own questions and create their own self-test flash cards
  • Additional learning resources are provided including OSCE scenarios and self-testing tools for learning anatomy and common drugs.
  • Access to all of the above is free for 24 hours, after which there is a charge of Â£10 for access for the rest of the academic year. All accounts reset in September.

This model has been very popular at KCL. More information can be found on our FAQs page.
We want to expand to other medical schools and are looking for enthusiastic students to help make the content relevant to your course.

If you want to become a site leader, we offer the following deal:

  • We provide the website and technical support, you provide the content and marketing for your university. You don't need any programming skills!
  • You are responsible for writing questions for your course, organised according to your course structure. You are welcome to use questions from our existing question banks, but they must be organised for your course.
  • In exchange, you will receive 50% of all profits from your site. We will also provide a certificate for your CV. And of course writing and reviewing questions will make you almost certain to pass your exams!
